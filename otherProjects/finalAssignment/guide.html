Heritage

Since the beginning we have focused on providing an exceptional product suited to the lifestyles and tastes of upper class North-American consumers. After extensive research done on the U.S. West coast, Toyota decided in 1985 that there was a need for seperate brand and sales channels were in order to properly cater to this consumer.

Our very first car was parised for its quietness, beautiful interior and ergonomically conscious interior styling, attentive engine performance, aerodynamics, fuel economy, value, and overall quality of build. These qualities were fitting of a car that cost over $1 billion USD to develop!

1990's

In its first full year of sales, Lexus managed to sell over 63,000 sedans in the U.S., later going on to export to other markets like Canada and the U.K. the following year. In 1991, Lexus launched its first sports coupe, the SC 400, which shared the sedan's powerful V8 engine. At the end of 1991, Lexus had already become the top selling import car in the United States. In 1999 the company recorded its one-millionthvehicle sold in the U.S. market.

2000's

In 2004 the company recorded its two-millionth U.S. vehicle sale. Also accomplished was the creation of the first ever luxury-branded production hybrid SUV. In 2005, Lexus completed an organizational seperation from its parent company Toyota.
